30 August 2021 Entry 1:

Hello world! I'm Hassaan, the developer behind onlinechargemaster.com. Without any formal training, my coding experience mainly consists of learning by doing. Whether Online Chargemaster reaches product-market fit or not, the skills and experiences I gain will be invaluable. History so far: In January 2021, Medicare promulgated 45 CFR §180.50. This rule means that hospitals have to post a chargemaster, which is a list of procedures/services and associated charges. This was meant to bring about price transparency, and with that, competition and the lower prices which would ensue. However, there are many complicating factors. What we address is ease of access. Finding the chargemaster for each hospital is difficult, and parsing the file is taxing. My software lets user input a CPT code for a specific procedure and get a list of the hospitals in their area that offer that procedure, ranked by price. Forward Roadmap: Currently at the v1.0.0, there's still a lot of work left and a lot of features I want to implement. Here's a short list:
Customizable range for local area
Prettier interface
Expand from California
Parse wider variety of chargemaster formats
Hospital reviews and ratings
Information on codes/procedures
Different prices for insured patients
Typable CPT input Ability to input procedure in natural language
Related procedures suggested to user
Simplicate and add more lightness
Of course, user feedback and interviews are essential. However, I want to make it less ugly before showing it to users. Hmm how about by October I need to have talked to at least three potential users? But who are the users? Well, right now the problem I'm trying to solve is when people go to the hospital, they have no idea what it will cost until they get a magical bill in the mail. This problem falls heaviest on self-paying folks. There aren't many of those people, proportionally, but perhaps social media could help me find a group of them. Also, insurance companies may find use from this as well, as it tells them the cheapest hospital, but don't they already know? Well I don’t need to know everything. I’ve heard that, when making decisions, it’s best to optimize for learning. So what will quickly teach me the most? User interviews, I think. To do that, I need to first make the interface a bit less ugly. And I need to find possible users.
